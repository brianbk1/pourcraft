About PourCraft

PourCraft is a tool for bar professionals: tell it what's behind the bar, what season it is, and what the drink is for, and it invents a completely original cocktail — never a variation on an existing recipe — with a spec sheet a bartender could actually put into service.

It's built for the person already behind the stick, not someone learning what a jigger is. Output assumes familiarity with bar terminology and technique — dry shakes, expressing oils, single versus double straining — and gives exact specs rather than home-kitchen explanations. The goal is a genuinely usable menu addition, not a novelty.

What You Can Control

  • Ingredients on hand — up to eight, used as the backbone of the recipe rather than a rigid ingredient list.
  • Season — spring, summer, autumn, or winter, shaping technique, garnish, and flavor direction.
  • Craft technique — fat washing, milk washing, oleo saccharum, smoking, rapid infusion, acid adjusting, carbonation, or saline enhancement, if you want the drink built around a specific method.
  • Target color — a color the finished drink should present in the glass, achieved through real ingredients rather than food dye.
  • Serving size — a single R&D sample, a 12-serving batch for a shift, or a 50-serving event build, with batching math and shelf-life notes scaled accordingly.
  • Occasion — happy hour, a tasting menu pairing, a holiday menu, brunch, late night, or a private event, which shapes strength, presentation, and pour cost thinking.
  • Kitchen context — tell it what the kitchen is currently serving and the food pairings get built around your actual menu, not generic suggestions.

Remixing

Once a drink is generated, it can be pushed in a direction — smokier, sweeter, stronger, lighter, more bitter, more herbal — while keeping its identity and name lineage intact. This is closer to how a bar team actually iterates a house cocktail than starting over from a blank prompt each time.

Zero-Proof Mode

A toggle removes alcohol from every ingredient in the build, useful for a genuinely thought-out zero-proof menu rather than a soda-and-juice afterthought.

What It Isn't

PourCraft doesn't replace a bar program's development process or a bartender's palate — it's a fast way to get a genuinely original starting point on paper, with real technique and a real spec, that a human still tastes, adjusts, and approves before it goes on a menu.
